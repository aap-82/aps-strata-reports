Q15Major Defects Identified Across the Expert Reports

Nine (9) separate expert / consultant reports are contained within the strata report documentation:

  1. AED Fire Safety Report — Report 10490.2 (20 February 2023). Author: Nathan Halstead (Registered Building Surveyor, BDC0161). Supplementary fire safety inspection covering approximately 50% of remaining sole occupancy units. Identified additional BCA non-compliances including fire door defects, penetration fire stopping deficiencies, gaps in fire-rated construction, and passive fire protection failures.

    AED Report 10490.2, pp.7–68 within the compiled PDF.

  2. Karsai Consultants Structural Report (2 May 2022). Inspections of exposed / corroding steel reinforcement in concrete elements across balconies, balustrades, and slab soffits. Identified chloride contamination and carbonation risks to reinforced concrete elements.

    Karsai Report within compiled PDF.

  3. Breezeway Finishes Assessment (2 May 2022). Water ingress into breezeways during rain / wind events. Saturated sand / cement bedding accelerating corrosion. Stainless steel lift surrounds exhibiting significant tea staining. Powder coat paint on door frames inadequately protected.

    Breezeway Finishes Assessment pp.4–14.

  4. Solutions in Engineering Annual Safety Report (17 May 2022). Moderate hazards: car park stormwater drain tripping hazard; car park kerbing tripping hazard.

    Solutions in Engineering Safety Report pp.4–6 and Implementation Plan p.24 of 35.

  5. RHM Consultants Supplementary Defect Report No. 1 (7 January 2022). Author: Peter Le BBCM. 34 units inspected. 41 defect items identified including corroded wire in concrete causing spalling, water penetration through aluminium windows, foul odours from drainage, ineffective toilet flushing, cracked tiles, damaged floorboards.

    Supplementary Defect Report PL1, pp.978–1092 of compiled PDF.

  6. Watermark Services Group Hydraulic Technical Report (4 August 2021). Defects in stormwater, sewer, roof drainage, potable water, hot water, recycled water, and fire hydrant systems. Non-compliant plumbing installations breaching AS/NZS 3500 standards.

    Watermark Report P.21.0014, pp.5–118.

  7. AED Fire Safety Report — Report 10490 (4 May 2021). Initial fire safety inspection. 108 pages of extensive BCA non-compliances including fire door certification defects, penetration fire stopping failures, fire damper non-compliance, IBS strip installation issues.

    AED Report 10490, pp.1–108.

  8. RHM Consultants Original Defect Report (7 May 2021). Authors: Peter Le / Matt Scolari. 34 of 36 residential units inspected. 42 defect items across 19 units plus 21 common area defects. Key defects: water penetration through aluminium windows (6 units), exposed / corroding steel reinforcement (6 units), steel door jamb corrosion (14 common foyers), water seepage from podium slab, premature paint deterioration.

    Defect Report MS1, pp.1093–1284 of compiled PDF.

  9. Ross Warner Mechanical Ventilation Report (October 2021). 29 of 136 apartments inspected. Defects: non-complying carpark ventilation, missing filters, non-complying bathroom / laundry exhaust, condensate drain issues, missing insulation, non-complying fire dampers.

    Warner Mechanical Report pp.7–11 and Annexures.

Unit 102 specific finding: The RHM Original Defect Report records for Unit 102 (Item 17.1): "No observed or reported common property building defects." (Source: RHM Defect Report MS1, p.45 — file page 237 of File 4.)
